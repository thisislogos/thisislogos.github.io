Purnell Hines

Creative exercise - designing for a fictional road surface marking specialist

When designing the logo of Purnell Hines, we wanted to keep the brand design minimal and professional looking. This was done with the play of negative spaces and colours in the middle of logotype to give just a hint of what the company does.⁣

Inspiration behind the name

Edgar Purnell Hooley was a Welsh inventor who invented tarmac.

Edward N. Hines, the chairman of the Wayne County’s Board of Roads, was first insist that there had to be a solid demarcation line on roads. He came to this conclusion after seeing a milk wagon leave a white trail along a road.⁣
